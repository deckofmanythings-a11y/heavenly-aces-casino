How to Play

Basic Craps — The Pass Line

Place a Pass Line bet before the come-out roll. If the shooter rolls a 7, you win even money. Any other number becomes the Point.

Once the Point is set, keep rolling. Hit the Point again and Pass Line wins. Roll a 7 before the Point and it's a seven-out — Pass Line loses. Back your Pass Line with Odds — a bet with no house edge.

Two Modes

Crapless Craps: Only a 7 wins on the come-out. Every other number (2–12) becomes the Point. Place or Buy all 10 numbers. No dark side.

Standard Craps: 7 or 11 wins on the come-out; 2, 3, or 12 loses. Box numbers are 4,5,6,8,9,10. Includes Don't Pass, Don't Come, and Lay bets.

Dark Side: Don't Pass wins when 2 or 3 rolls on come-out (12 is a push), and wins when the shooter sevens out. Don't Come works the same way after the point. Lay bets win when 7 comes before the number. Back DP/DC with Lay Odds for no-vig true odds.

DP and DC are not contract bets — you can remove them at any time, but Lay Odds will be wiped if you reduce the flat bet.
